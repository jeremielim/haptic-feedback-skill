# Phase 2 — Exploration

Generate breadth before judging. Use the naming brief as the source of truth and preserve the raw
pool so later filtering remains auditable.

## Exploration Sequence

1. Extract raw material from the purpose, promise, audience, feeling, tone, category vocabulary,
   history, founders, geography, and product behavior.
2. Turn each promising word into several springboards: broader theme, concrete object, person,
   place, story, material, action, sound, or metaphor.
3. Generate across descriptive, experiential, evocative, invented, and identity-based name types.
4. Explore meaningful facets and real things before relying on misspellings or arbitrary suffixes.
5. Use modifications selectively: compounds, portmanteaus, spelling shifts, dropped vowels, and
   category-appropriate endings.
6. Categorize the pool as `promising`, `interesting`, or `raw`; defer elimination to Phase 3.

Examples of springboarding:

- Relaxed → pillow → cool side
- Futuristic → science fiction → authors → Asimov
- Calm → sleep → night sky → Orion

For features and screens, emphasize clear action verbs and extensible interface metaphors. For
design systems, explore company-linked language plus territories such as natural systems, materials,
geometry, tools, light, architecture, and navigation.

Use the candidate ranges in `SKILL.md` to calibrate volume. A smaller pool is complete only when it
still spans several distinct territories and name types.

## Output

Provide a categorized candidate pool, grouped by territory and name type. Include a short note on
the idea behind non-obvious names; keep scoring and ranking out of this phase.

Phase 2 is complete when the pool meets the calibrated volume, covers every selected territory, and
contains enough variety that the next phase can cut rather than invent.
