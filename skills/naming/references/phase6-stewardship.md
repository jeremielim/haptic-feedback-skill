# Phase 6 — Stewardship

Turn the chosen name into a coherent, maintained naming system.

## Stewardship Decisions

Document:

1. **Canonical form** — spelling, capitalization, pronunciation, possessive, abbreviation, and
   translations or transliterations.
2. **Founding story** — a concise, truthful explanation of the name's origin and meaning.
3. **Usage** — approved descriptors, introductory language, URL and handle conventions, and examples
   in product, marketing, support, and internal communication.
4. **Architecture** — how parent brand, products, plans, features, components, campaigns, and future
   extensions relate.
5. **Ownership** — who approves new names, maintains the registry, and monitors legal or market risk.
6. **Review triggers** — new markets, new product families, major repositioning, conflicts, or
   persistent user confusion.

For a design system or component library, include code, package, Figma, documentation, and spoken-use
forms. Favor a family resemblance across related names instead of unrelated metaphors.

## Output

Produce a stewardship guide containing:

- Name story and decision rationale
- Canonical usage rules and realistic examples
- Product-family or feature-naming architecture
- Reserved and prohibited patterns
- Owner, registry location, and approval process
- Internationalization notes and future review triggers

Phase 6 is complete when another team member can apply the name consistently, extend the naming
system, and know when and from whom to request a decision.
