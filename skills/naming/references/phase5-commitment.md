# Phase 5 — Commitment

Help the decision-maker choose against the naming brief and validation evidence. Clarity of ownership
matters more than committee consensus.

## Decision Brief

For every finalist, provide:

- Best unaided user-testing evidence
- Rank against the other finalists
- Fit with purpose, promise, feeling, tone, and range
- Legal, linguistic, domain, handle, and competitive status
- Two or three strongest arguments for the name
- Honest case against it
- Reversible next step and remaining uncertainty

When a group is involved, identify one decision-maker, time-box deliberation, collect initial votes
independently, and use discussion to surface evidence rather than average preferences. The
decision-maker makes the call.

If exposure would help, place each finalist in realistic mockups or daily-use surfaces for several
days. Treat post-decision anxiety as a prompt to identify a concrete defect, not automatic evidence
that the choice is wrong.

## After the Decision

Recommend an action sequence without executing external actions unless the user asks:

1. Obtain qualified trademark clearance and plan any filing.
2. Acquire the chosen domain or select the documented fallback.
3. Secure relevant social handles.
4. Record the name story and the rationale for the decision.

## Output

Produce comparable decision briefs, a recommendation grounded in the naming brief, and one of two
outcomes:

- `chosen`: name the selection, rationale, remaining risks, owner, and next actions; or
- `blocked`: name each item of missing evidence or unresolved tradeoff and how to resolve it.

Phase 5 is complete when the decision-maker can choose from comparable evidence or every remaining
blocker has been made explicit.
